DNAVR (Unreal Engine)

Description

This project is our capstone project for EECS 498: XR and Society. My team and I were tasked with creating a socially impactful AR/VR app. We chose to create a VR game to teach high school students about the DNA transcription/translation process. Students are responsible for building an mRNA strand by matching nucleotide bases to a strand of DNA, bringing the mRNA to the ribosome, building the protein encoded in the mRNA bringing tRNA to the ribosome to match codon/anticodon pairs, and finally packaging the protein and delivering it to the cell wall.

Features

  • 3D movement throughout a cell model
  • Guided tutorial system to tell the player what to do next
  • Information cards for each organelle/game object so students can learn about cellular functions
  • Grabbable nucleotide bases (A/C/T/G/U)
  • An inventory system for storing extra nucleotide bases
  • Ability to snap a nucleotide base to the correct matching base on a strand of DNA
  • mRNA strand pops off from the DNA strand after it has been successfully built by the player
  • Players can take the mRNA strand to a ribosome to begin the translation process
  • tRNA preferentially spawns based on which codon/anticodon pair is next in the translation process
  • Amino acid chain grows gradually as players bring the correct tRNA over to the ribosome
  • Amino chain becomes grabbable once the translation process is complete
  • Players can bring the amino acid chain to the Golgi Apparatus to package the protein
  • Packaged proteins can be brought to the cell wall to earn "lysocoins" (from the word "lysosome")
  • Virus attack functionality: once players build and deliver 2 proteins, a virus will spawn, releasing toxins into the cell which decrease the spawning frequency of nucleotide bases, hindering their progress
  • Lysosomes can be bought with "lysocoins" which actively seek out and destroy the virus toxins
  • Classroom starting level with a leaderboard for the best time to build 3 proteins
